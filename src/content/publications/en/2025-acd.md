abstract: "Zero-shot human-AI coordination trains an ego-agent to coordinate with humans without human data. Most prior work focuses on improving coordination with unseen co-players but ignores generalization to unseen environments. This paper extends multi-agent UED (Unsupervised Environment Design) to zero-shot human-AI coordination by proposing a return-based utility function and prioritized co-player sampling. Evaluated in the Overcooked-AI environment with real humans (N=20), the method outperforms baseline approaches on all evaluation layouts, achieving higher collaborativeness ratings and human preference scores."
